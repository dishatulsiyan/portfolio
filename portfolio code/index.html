Too Lazy to Cook-Book

For this project, I created a typographic cookbook, Too Lazy to Cook-Book, aimed at young adults (18–25) who want quick, easy, and fun recipes.

Ideation

The theme evolved from brainstorming relatable concepts for beginner cooks. I focused on a quirky, approachable style that resonates with young, lazy, and broke individuals, combining humor and practicality to make cooking enjoyable.

Design Process

I incorporated playful, food-inspired typography to reflect the theme and connect visuals with content. Vibrant colors and custom illustrations enhanced each section, like Morning Zombie and One-Pot Wonders, adding personality while keeping it functional.

Layout Exploration

Recipe pages were designed for clarity and engagement, featuring side-by-side ingredients, instructions, and playful icons. I experimented with text alignment, curved text, and dynamic fonts to ensure a lively yet organized aesthetic.

Cover Concept

The cover design uses bread as a central motif, symbolizing simplicity and ease—perfect for beginners. This metaphor aligns with the cookbook’s mission to make cooking accessible and fun.

The final outcome is a vibrant, functional cookbook that balances creativity with practicality, perfect for its target audience.

Glossary on Beauty Standards in Art History

This project involved designing a glossary exploring beauty standards throughout European art history, spanning eras like Rococo, Romanticism, Neoclassism etc. The design reflects the elegance and opulence of these periods.

Design Elements

The cover features intricate floral motifs and a lavish pattern inspired by European decorative arts. These elements set the tone for the theme, celebrating beauty as a timeless concept across art movements.

Typography

We used blackletter fonts for the drop letters, embellished with floral motifs to evoke European elegance and historical sophistication. The combination of typography and decoration reinforces the glossary's focus on beauty standards in art.

Color Theme

A muted orange and rust color palette was chosen to reflect warmth, richness, and the timeless charm of historical European art. These tones create a cohesive and inviting aesthetic while complementing the intricate patterns and motifs.

The final glossary is a visually engaging and conceptually rich piece, seamlessly blending historical context with artistic beauty.

The Three Arrows of Fate: Barbarik’s Journey

This project is a digitally illustrated storybook inspired by the story of Barbarik, a significant figure from the Mahabharata. The narrative delves into the themes of loyalty, sacrifice, and destiny, exploring Barbarik’s pivotal role in the great epic.

Concept & Theme

The storybook combines Indian mythology with a bold visual narrative style. The illustrations depict characters as black silhouettes against vibrant, colorful backgrounds. This approach emphasizes the drama and mystery of Barbarik’s tale while paying homage to traditional Indian art forms.

Visual Style

  • Silhouette Characters: Black silhouettes create a striking contrast, highlighting the actions and emotions of the characters without intricate detailing.
  • Vibrant Backgrounds: Each page features dynamic, colorful backgrounds that reflect the mood and intensity of the story’s key moments.
  • Indian Motifs: Patterns and motifs inspired by traditional Indian art enhance the authenticity and cultural richness of the visuals.

Narrative Approach

The story is narrated through a combination of illustrated frames and concise text, drawing readers into the journey of Barbarik—his extraordinary powers, his moral dilemmas, and his ultimate sacrifice. The structure follows key episodes:

  1. Barbarik’s inheritance of the three divine arrows.
  2. His vow to always fight for the weaker side.
  3. The meeting with Krishna, which leads to the revelation of his destined sacrifice.

Cultural Context

The project bridges mythology and modern storytelling, making the tale accessible and engaging for a contemporary audience. It celebrates the timeless wisdom of Indian epics while experimenting with unique, graphic storytelling techniques.

Wanderer’s Peak Wine Bottle Poster

This poster design connects deeply to Romanticism, a 19th-century movement centered on the sublime, exploring the beauty and terror of nature and human vulnerability within it. Inspired by Caspar David Friedrich’s *Wanderer Above the Sea of Fog,* the poster captures themes of introspection and the search for meaning. The lone figure atop a misty cliff symbolizes personal reflection and the grandeur of the unknown, aligning with the wine’s identity as an emotional, personal journey.

Typography

The brand name, “Wanderer’s Peak,” is set in a flowing serif font, evoking elegance and timelessness akin to Romantic literature. The tagline, “In the mist, the soul finds its way,” complements the painting’s introspective essence.

Poster Design

The wine bottle is central, blending seamlessly into Friedrich’s landscape to unify the Romantic theme. Additional text on the sides elaborates on the wine’s inspiration and drinking experience without distracting from the visual focus.

Borders & Decoration

Subtle ornate borders and decorative corner elements create a vintage feel, reminiscent of 19th-century art. Organic textures enhance the natural and timeless aesthetic, completing the Romantic connection.

Starry Bites Pizza Box Poster

The *Starry Bites* pizza box design takes inspiration from the vibrant and emotive strokes of Post-Impressionism, drawing directly from Vincent van Gogh’s *Starry Night.* The swirling skies of the iconic painting are reimagined to depict the dynamic, swirling toppings of a pizza, turning the box into a canvas of taste and visual intrigue. The design invites customers to “Taste the Masterpiece,” evoking the awe of viewing an artwork while enjoying a culinary delight.

Typography

The chosen font, Brush Script MT Italic, complements the artistic theme with its flowing, expressive style. Its hand-painted feel mirrors the emotive brushstrokes of Post-Impressionism, enhancing the overall creative presentation.

Imagery

Swirling patterns and vibrant colors inspired by Van Gogh’s *Starry Night* represent cosmic energy and emotional depth, blending the essence of fine art with the delight of pizza. This artistic approach transforms the pizza box into a memorable and visually striking experience.

Helvetica-Inspired Typographic Poster
This typographic poster is inspired by the work of graphic designer Josef Müller-Brockmann, known for his use of precise, mathematical grid systems. Following his approach, I utilized a diagonal grid, carefully calculated at specific angles, to structure the design. The color palette of black, white, and red mirrors Müller-Brockmann's own, aligning with his minimalist aesthetic and emphasis on clarity and precision.

Design Inspiration

Josef Müller-Brockmann’s work exemplifies the principles of Helvetica, a typeface he often used for its simplicity and legibility. His focus on minimalist, functional design was a perfect match for Helvetica's strengths, and I aimed to create a powerful visual communication through these elements.

Pasta Packaging Design

For this pasta packaging, I created a custom font that reflects the authenticity and simplicity of traditional Italian cuisine. The font’s elegant curves and organic shapes evoke the natural beauty of fresh ingredients, while maintaining readability and modern appeal. The packaging design balances contemporary minimalism with a nod to rustic, homemade pasta, emphasizing quality and tradition.

Design Concept

The typography, crafted specifically for this project, plays a central role in conveying the product’s artisanal nature. I focused on creating a design that feels both timeless and approachable, connecting with consumers seeking a genuine culinary experience. The packaging’s color palette—warm, earthy tones—enhances the product's authentic appeal, inviting customers to experience the rich flavors inside.

Process

The name “Gioggio’s” was chosen to evoke an authentic Italian feel, paying homage to Italy’s rich culinary heritage. The double “G” in the name not only adds a playful, rhythmic quality but also visually resembles the bow-tie shape of Farfalle pasta. This clever design choice ties the brand identity closely to the product itself, creating a cohesive and memorable brand image.

For the background pattern, I used flipped “G”s, which also serve as the logo of the brand, reinforcing the connection between the product and its unique identity. This creates an artisanal and charming aesthetic, highlighting the handcrafted, organic essence of the pasta.
